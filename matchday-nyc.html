Where it happens

The final is at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey — about 8–10 miles (13–16 km) west of Midtown Manhattan, inside the Meadowlands Sports Complex. For the tournament it's officially called "New York New Jersey Stadium." It's an open-air bowl, so plan for the weather.

🎤 A first in history

The final features the first-ever World Cup halftime show, Super Bowl–style, with a star headline lineup. Confirm the latest details before you go.

☀️ July in New York

Hot and humid — highs around 30°C / 86°F+. Dress light, wear sunscreen, and carry water. The stadium is uncovered.

🎟️
One golden rule: a valid FIFA match ticket is required for almost everything on the day — including buying stadium transport. Sort your ticket first, then your travel.
02

Before You Fly

Sort the boring-but-critical stuff early — visas and embassy slots don't wait.

🛂 ESTA or visa

From a Visa Waiver country (most of Europe, the UK, Australia, Japan, South Korea, New Zealand and more)? You need an approved ESTA before you fly — apply only at the official site esta.cbp.dhs.gov (avoid lookalike sites that overcharge), at least 72 hours ahead. The fee is about $40; it's valid 2 years, multiple entries, up to 90 days a visit. Not from a Visa Waiver country? You'll need a B1/B2 visitor visa, and embassy appointments can take months — start now.

🏥
Get travel insurance with medical cover. US healthcare is brutally expensive — a single ER visit can run into the thousands. Don't fly without it.

🛃 Passport & arrival

Bring an e-passport valid for ~6 months beyond your stay. At immigration, have your return ticket and hotel address ready, and expect a photo and fingerprints. Declare cash over $10,000; don't bring fresh food, meat or plants.

💳 Cards, power & phone

Tell your bank you're travelling so your card isn't blocked, and check foreign-transaction fees. The US runs on 120V, Type A/B plugs — pack an adapter. Load your FIFA ticket in the official app, save an offline map, and sort a US eSIM before you land.

03

Get to the Stadium

This is the part that trips fans up. Read it twice — and book ahead.

🚫
There is NO general parking at the stadium on match day. Access is by official transport only, and you must hold a valid match ticket to buy it. Walk-up parking, charters and private buses are off the table. Plan and pre-book.
ANJ Transit Train Best for most▾
≈ $105 round trip

The most reliable route. From NY Penn Station, ride to Secaucus Junction (~10 min), then transfer to the match-day Meadowlands shuttle train straight to the stadium. ~45–60 min door-to-door from Midtown.

  • Buy in advance in the NJ Transit mobile app. Match-day rail tickets are capped (about 40,000/day) and are not sold at stations on the day.
  • Service starts roughly 4 hours before kickoff. From that point, Penn ↔ Secaucus is limited to fans with match tickets.
  • Coming from elsewhere in NJ? Connect to Secaucus (or via Hoboken) and join the same shuttle.
BOfficial Stadium Shuttle▾
≈ $20 round trip

Direct round-trip coach from several New York hubs and a New Jersey park-and-ride — one seat, no transfer at Secaucus.

  • Must be booked in advance; match ticket required; allocations are limited and sell out.
  • Great value and simple — secure it when you arrange your ticket and hotel.
CUber / Rideshare▾

Possible, but the least predictable option on final day.

  • No drop-off on stadium property. Cars use a designated zone at Meadowlands Racing & Entertainment, then it's roughly a 1 mile (1.3 km) walk.
  • Expect surcharges and a long, chaotic pickup queue after the whistle.
DDriving & Parking▾
Last resort

No general parking exists. The only pre-bookable option is American Dream (around $225, match ticket required), with a walk via the pedestrian bridge. Surrounding roads gridlock for hours — avoid unless you truly need a car.

✈️ From the airport

Newark (EWR) is closest to the stadium. To Manhattan: AirTrain → NJ Transit to Penn Station (~30 min). JFK: AirTrain → subway, or the LIRR into Penn (~50–70 min). LaGuardia (LGA): AirTrain/subway, or a bus + subway (~45–60 min). A JFK–Manhattan taxi is a flat fare; EWR and LGA are metered.

Land the day before the final, not on match day — and remember you can't bring luggage on the stadium train.

🆘 If the rail is disrupted

Have a plan B. NY Waterway ferries run from Manhattan to Hoboken, and the PATH train also links Manhattan to Hoboken — from there you can pick up stadium-bound trains. Handy, since on match days Penn ↔ Secaucus is limited to ticket holders.

⏱ Timing: aim to be at the stadium ~3 hours before kickoff — security can take 45+ minutes and gates open about 2 hours early. Bring as little as possible; expect a clear-bag policy (confirm on the official site).
🔙 Getting back: 70,000+ fans leave at once. Don't sprint for the exit — wait 30–45 min, grab a drink, let it thin. Extra trains run for 2–3 hours after full time.
04

At the Stadium

Security is strict and confiscated items are not returned. Get this right before you leave your hotel.

🎒
Clear-bag policy. Only a clear plastic/PVC bag up to 12" × 6" × 12" (30×15×30 cm), plus one small non-clear clutch up to 4.5" × 6.5". No backpacks of any kind — you'll be turned away regardless of what's inside. The same rule applies on the match trains and shuttles, so you can't bring luggage.

✅ What you can bring

Phone, a power bank (stadium WiFi dies under 80,000+ people — a dead phone at the gate is a real problem), a small clutch, and toiletries/sunscreen under 100ml each. Load your ticket in the official app and fully charge your phone before you arrive.

🚫 Leave at the hotel

Backpacks and large or non-clear bags, suitcases, hard/reusable water bottles, lighters, matches, flares, aerosols and spray cans, pro cameras with long lenses, drones, and anything with a rigid pole. Confiscated items are not returned.

🏟️ Inside the stadium

It's a cashless venue — concessions take card or phone only, no cash. Food and drink are pricey (think $15+ a beer), so eat before you go. Restroom lines are worst at halftime, so go before the whistle. There's no re-entry once you leave, and no smoking or vaping inside. Need accessible seating or help? Contact stadium guest services in advance.

💧 Water & heat: bottle rules have changed more than once — leave hard/reusable bottles at home and check the official policy right before you go. There are hydration and cooling stations inside. It's open-air in July: hat, sunscreen, light clothes.
🏬 Travelling between cities? You can't take a suitcase to the stadium or on the match train. Luggage-storage services near the stadium and in Manhattan start around $5/bag — drop it and travel light.

07

Where to Stay

It comes down to one trade-off: city buzz vs. easy stadium access. Book early — final weekend fills fast and prices spike.

🏙 Manhattan (Midtown)

The full NYC experience and closest to Penn Station for the stadium run. Priciest — book now.

🌉 Brooklyn / Long Island City

Cooler neighborhoods, a bit cheaper, quick subway hop to Penn. Great base for foodies.

🚆 New Jersey (Secaucus / Jersey City / Hoboken)

Right on the transit route, often better value, and a fast connection to Secaucus on match day.

💸 Budget hack

Stay further out — or even Philadelphia — and train in. Cheaper rooms, longer commute. Worth it if money's tight.

08

Booking 101

Lock things in early — and in this order. Final weekend sells out and prices climb.

💸 Rough costs (final weekend, per person)

Match tickets and flights vary wildly, so beyond those budget roughly: hotel $250–500+/night in Manhattan on final weekend; stadium rail ~$105 round trip (or ~$20 shuttle); subway $3/ride; food $40–90/day; attractions $30–45 each. New York is pricey — keep a buffer.

1 · 🎟️ Match ticket

Buy only through official FIFA channels (including official resale). Avoid third-party scalpers and fakes — a bad ticket can mean no entry and no transport.

2 · ✈️ Flights

Compare on Google Flights. Newark (EWR) is closest to the stadium; JFK and LaGuardia (LGA) also work. Book early and aim to land the day before the final.

3 · 🏨 Hotel

Compare on Google Hotels and Booking.com; consider Airbnb for groups. Pick free-cancellation rates near a subway line or Penn Station. Note NYC hotel tax (~14%+) and any extra fees on top of the nightly rate.

4 · 🚆 Stadium transport

Book match-day rail in the NJ Transit app, or grab an official shuttle — both are capped and sell out. Do it the moment you have your match ticket.

5 · 🎫 Attractions

Book timed entry for the Statue of Liberty and observation decks ahead. If you'll see a lot, a city pass (CityPASS / Go City) can save money.

NYC Survival Kit

The practical stuff that makes you look like a local.

🚇 Subway
$3 a ride. Just tap a contactless card or phone (OMNY) — no need to buy a MetroCard. Fastest way around Manhattan.
💵 Tipping
15–20% at restaurants, bars and taxis is expected here — it's part of workers' pay. Counter spots are optional.
💳 Money
Cards and Apple/Google Pay work nearly everywhere. Carry a little cash for small vendors and tips.
📶 Data
Grab a US eSIM before you fly so maps and your tickets work the moment you land.
☀️ Weather
July is hot and humid (~30°C/86°F+). Light clothes, sunscreen, water, and a layer for heavy AC indoors.
🛟 Safety
Tourist areas are generally safe; keep normal big-city awareness and watch your bag in crowds.
📱 Apps
Download MTA (subway), NJ Transit (your stadium tickets!), Google Maps, and your FIFA ticket wallet.
🆘 Emergencies
Dial 911 for police, fire or medical emergencies anywhere in the US.
💲 Tax & tips on top
Listed prices exclude ~9% sales tax (added at the register) and you tip on top — the sticker price isn't the final price.
🍺 Drinking age 21
You must be 21 to drink and you'll be ID'd everywhere — carry your passport or ID.
🏠 Airbnb caution
NYC bans most whole-home short stays (Local Law 18). Many "entire apartment" listings are illegal and can be cancelled last-minute — book a hotel, or only a registered listing where the host lives on-site.
🚰 Tap water
It's safe and free — ask for it at restaurants instead of buying bottles.
💊 Pharmacies
CVS, Walgreens and Duane Reade are everywhere for medicine, sunscreen and basics.
🛡 Common scams
Ignore "free" CDs/bracelets and costumed characters in Times Square who then demand money. Buy tickets only from official sources.

Quick FAQ

The questions every first-timer asks.

Do I need a car?▾
No — it's the worst option. There's no general parking and roads gridlock for hours. Take the train or official shuttle.
Can I buy the stadium train ticket on the day?▾
No. Match-day rail must be bought ahead in the NJ Transit app; it's capped and not sold at stations on the day. Buy it the moment you have your match ticket.
How early should I arrive?▾
About 3 hours before kickoff. Security can take 45+ minutes and gates open ~2 hours early.
Is New York safe?▾
Tourist areas are generally safe. Use normal big-city awareness, watch your bag in crowds, and stick to busy, well-lit streets at night.
Cash or card?▾
Cards and Apple/Google Pay work almost everywhere, including the subway (tap OMNY). Carry a little cash for small vendors and tips.
What if it rains?▾
The stadium is open-air and the match plays on. Pack a thin poncho (umbrellas are usually restricted). July is more often hot and humid than wet.
How do I get back after the final?▾
70,000+ fans leave at once. Wait 30–45 minutes, grab a drink, then ride back — extra trains run for 2–3 hours after the whistle.
Do I need an ESTA or a visa?▾
From a Visa Waiver country (most of Europe, UK, Australia, Japan, Korea, etc.) you need an approved ESTA — apply at esta.cbp.dhs.gov ahead of time (~$40). Otherwise you'll need a visitor visa, so start early.
Is the stadium cashless?▾
Yes — concessions take card or phone only, no cash. Bring a contactless card or your phone.
Can I leave and re-enter?▾
No. There's no re-entry once you exit, so don't head out until you're done.
Is Airbnb legal in NYC?▾
Most whole-home short stays are banned (Local Law 18). Book a hotel, or only a registered hosted stay where the owner lives on-site — illegal listings get cancelled.
